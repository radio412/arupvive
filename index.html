Secret Instructions

Setting up the VR Rig

This guide is focused on the case layout and unboxing of the Vive. For questions about details such as light indicators, software, and features see the Steam VR Support Guide.

STOP

    I know you're excited. You want to setup the Vive RIGHT NOW. Take a deep breath and read this document. Click the link to the Steam Setup Guide above, and read it. Then begin setting up the Vive.

    Take this guide slowly. Imagine you're setting up a climbing wall and all the ropes, connectors, and clips must be perfect to ensure the safety of everyone particpating. Now blindfold your climbers and think about what risks may be present. The Vive experience is immersive and injury is a real risk. It is common for some Vive experiences to cause loss of breath and imbalance even in very fit individuals!

    You're creating a world to step into. Be sure to do this with plenty of rest. Awareness of your surroundings is key. Ask yourself questions: Is the area large enough for the Vive? Can I setup extension cords, connections and other equipment in a safe manner? Will people trip over cords or become entangled? When the user starts jumping and swinging around the room, will they hit anything or other people?

    If a sensor falls, it WILL break. If you or the user falls, you will break. Safety comes first. Chandeliers, light bulbs, furniture and people have all been damaged in the course of learning and understanding the Vive. Users of the Vive become animated, physical and disconnected from the real world. As steward of the virtual experience you must setup a safe environment for those who will use the Vive. Ove Arup, in the Key Speech said nothing of safety. Because he assumed we aren't dumb. Don't be dumb.

    Whenever possible, use the buddy system.

1. Open the case

  • Put the case on the ground facing you
  • Flip open the latches all of the way
  • Lift up the lid

The combination is obtained via the introductory email.

The storage of the Vive equipment requires precision. The layout and position of items is crucial to getting it back in the box. Take a moment to study the contents without removing them yet.

2. Take out the laptop and power supply

  • Both are located in the lid
  • Flip up the Velcro cover and take out the laptop
  • Unzip the pouch and take out the power supply

The Vive is connected to the laptop via a cable. Because the cable can pull on the equipment, keep the laptop low, or on the floor. Be sure to keep it in a place where you won't step on it, and outside the Vive setup perimeter.

3. Take out sensor tripods and extension cords

  • Both are in the front section of the case
  • Note how the extension cords are layered in around the tripods, it is crucial to carefully unpack and pack them together or the case will not close
  • Take out both tripods
  • Take out both extensiton cords

The tripods and extension cords were packed specifically to fit in the case. There is only one way they fit. Use the photos in the notes section at the bottom of this document to see how they fit in the box.

4. Setup Tripods

  • First extend down the legs and lock, they should be flat on the floor
  • Extend up each section of the tripod and lock, starting with largest to smallest sections, be careful not to over tighten
  • Make sure the weight is on the three pads, and not the center of the stand, or the tripod will wobble
  • Do the same for both
  • Put the tripods in opposite corners of the “play area”, ideally 4-5 meters (15’) apart

The legs of the tripods are unfolded to be flat as shown. This is the most stable configuration of the tripod. Do not set the legs at an angle and only use them on a flat, even floor.

5. Double check all knobs on tripods for tightness

  • Be careful not to over-tighten
  • Make sure the washers are behind the knobs, or they will not work and hold
  • Make sure nothing is loose

Be sure the washers are in place. Without the washers, the knob can be overturned and damaged in attempting to fit it tightly. Be sure not to lose the washers if the knob is accidentally removed completey.

6. Plug in sensor extension cords

  • Note where power outlets are in the room
  • Put the tripods in corners so power can run to the base of the tripods

Think about your Vive work area. A diagonal line across the space must allow at least 16.5 feet between the tripods. If you have power cords which must cross your work space, use gaffing tape to ensure Vive users will not trip on them and injure themselves or cause the tripods to fall over.

7. Remove sensors

  • Use the black and white hounds-tooth ribbon to gently pull the sensors up and out of the narrow sensor storage area
  • Note that one is facing up and the other is facing down and that the plastic-covered faces touch each other
  • Once out of the case, remove the protective plastic covers and place the covers back in the case with the hounds-tooth ribbon

When placing the sensors back in the case, don't forget to put the ribbon in place so it can be used to unpack the sensors later.

These covers protect the sensors. Keep track of them and don't place them on the floor.

Place the protective covers in the case when they are not attached to a sensor.

8. Take out main power extension cord

  • It is in the top left corner storage area
  • This is the big one that also has the ground plug, it is used for the computer and the base station
  • Plug into the wall and place on the ground where the computer will be set up

This extension cord is a different size than the ones used by the sensors. They are not interchangeable. Always place the larger primary extension cord in the top left corner of the case.

Note the difference between the primary extension cord and the sensor extension cords.

9. Take out and install the tripod / sensor mounts

  • Both are in the top left corner storage area
  • Mount mounts on top of tripods first (trust it is a lot easier)
  • Mount sensors on mounts
  • Point the face of the sensor towards the opposite sensor (they have to “see” each other)
  • Angle the sensor slightly towards the floor

The mounts should already be set to an ideal angle, but you will need to check the angle as you set up the sensors.

DO NOT try to put the mounts directly on the sensors. This will make mounting them on the tripods difficult.

Attach the mounts to the top of the tripods. Be sure not to cross thread them. They should go on smoothly and tighten easily.

Be sure the mounts are secure.

Now attach the sensors. Always hold the sensor until you know it is secure.

A wingnut on the mount secures the sensor by tightening towards the sensor.

The friction adjustment allows you to pivot the mount. Be sure it is positioned before moving on.

The resulting mount allows a proper 120 degree angle towards the floor.

Double check all fittings before walking away

Inspect the area. Be sure all wires, tripod legs and equipment are out of the way. Be sure not to snag any wires which could pull the tripods onto the floor.

10. Power the sensors

  • Take out the three grey power cords from the top left corner storage area
  • Identify the two with the longer and thicker cords, these are the ones for the sensors
  • Note how they are neatly wrapped, they need to wrapped this way in order to fit back into the case during pack-up
  • Plug cord into the back of the sensor
  • Plug the power side into the extension cord at the base of the tripod
  • The sensor should then light up with a light in the top middle, and, show either the letter “b” or “c” (and should not be set to “A”). The switch is in the back in case it needs to be changed.
  • Note that the there is a motorized sensor within that spins and causes the case to vibrate. No cause for alarm.

Note the position of the sensor power supplies. They must be packed specifically to allow all items to fit back into the case.

The power supplies are identical and interchangeable.

Another unit and pwoer cord are in the case. Leave it in the case for now.

Plug the sensor power cords into the back of the sensors.

Find a suitable wall outlet for each sensor, plug them in.

A blue light at the top means the sensor is still booting up. A purple light means the sensor cannot yet locate the other sensor or the laptop.

When setup is complete all indicators will be green.

11. Set up the Super Bad Ass Alienware Computer

  • Plug in power supply to computer
  • Plug in power supply to extension cord

Note the location of the primary Link Box and cord.

The Link Box sits between the Vive, Sensors and Laptop. Never connect sensors or the Vive headset HDMI directly to the Laptop!

12. Plug in the Vive Hub (Sometimes called the Link Box by HTC) to the computer and power

  • Connect the USB cable to the side port on the computer (either side is fine)
  • Connect the USB cable to GREY USB port on the Hub (not the one with an orange ring)
  • Connect the HDMI to the back on the computer
  • Connect the other side of the HDMI cable to GREY HDMI port on the Hub (not the one with orange ring)
  • Connect the 12V power cable (the third power cable from the top left corner storage area) into the GREY 12V port on the HUB (and not the one with the orange ring)
  • Plug the other side into the main extension cord in which the computer power supply is also powered

The Link Box has indicators cast right into the housing and is color coded on the sides. This can be confusing still, but HTC has put little hints like this to help you out. Take it slow and understand each piece you are connecting before connecting them.

Don't forget to plugin the laptop. The Vive uses all of the laptop's capabilities and uses power fast.

Connect the HDMI and USB to the laptop accordingly. This isn't rocket science, but can go wrong. Be gentle with all connections. Use hand moisturizer and avoid the nail conditions shown here.

Enjoy some beer if you can. USB plugs into the side of the laptop.

Notice how the things that plug into the laptop go on the side of the Link Box that has NO orange coloring.

HDMI plugs into the back of the laptop.

13. Remove the headset from the case

  • Take out and place the following to the side:
    • The inter-pupillary measurement sheet (more on this later)
    • The small-size headset pad (in plastic)
    • The screen cleaning cloth (in plastic)
  • The headphones / earbuds (lots of sets in plastic cases)
    • Carefully take out the headset
    • NEVER have fingers touch the inside screen
    • Unwind the cable like you are unspooling it from a reel (this takes out all kinks and makes it easier for gameplay)

Don't lose these instructions. Measure users' eyes, perfect the experience.

Be gentle, and don't touch the inside of the headset.

keep the wiress neatly coiled when not in use. Keep your workspace organized.

14. Connect headset to Vive Hub (Sometimes called the Link Box by HTC)

  • Connect the USB to the orange-ringed USB port on the Hub
  • Connect the HDMI to the orange-ringed HDMI port on the Hub
  • Connect the 12V power to the orange-ringed power port on the Hub

The headset's wires connect to the Link Box on the VR side.

Note the difference between the PC and VR side.

15. Plug in earbuds or headphones

  • Take out headphones / earbuds from pouch (or, use your preferred headphones)
  • Plug them into the headphone jack on the back of the headset
  • NOTE: one channel may not work once in use, wiggle and make sure the headphones are well connected to the jack to get both channels to work)

You can use any headphones you like, but make sure the wires and plug are secure and do not impede the user, or clutter the space.

16. Take out the controllers

  • Carefully remove the two controllers from the center rectangle compartment in the case
  • NOTE: The controllers are inter-locked within the storage compartment, note how this is done so they may put back in a similar manner
  • Take them out of their storage bags
  • Put the storage bags back into the storage area
  • NOTE: the power charging cables for the controllers are also in the compartment. Use these as-needed to charge the controllers

The controllers are in netted bags to keep them from getting damaged. Keep them in the bags until you're ready.

Press the clip to release the string. Do not force the string.

The controllers are your best friend. When were they charged last? Will they work for the duration you need them to? Don't be lame, charge them before putting them back in the box.

Turning the controllers on is this button. The icon and placement doesn't make any sense, but this is the button.

17. GET THAT SHIT GOING ON!

18. A few notes on the art of repacking

  • Controllers – make sure they interlock correctly
  • Headset storage area:
    • Carefully coil the headset cable as if you are coiling around a tight spool – It will only make it into the case if it is carefully and tightly coiled correctly
    • Make sure the headset goes in facing the bottom of the case
    • Make sure the items that get packed with the headset do not touch the screen lens or can fall into the headset lens area
  • Power cables, Hub, and connection cables
    • Carefully wrap the cables for the sensor power cords and the Hub power cable with their velcro straps
    • Stack the power plug side of each cable together so the Hub can slide into the gap
    • Put in the power cords first, then the hub and its cords, then the main extension cord
  • Computer – make sure the screen lid is facing you when you put it into the sleeve
  • Tripods and extension cords
    • Have all of the knobs for each vertical section line up
    • One of the three tripod legs is proud (or sticks out more), make sure this leg faces the bottom of the case when putting the first tripod into the bottom of the case. Also, make sure that the top brass nut is facing to the left side of the case (so the little wheel bump in the bottom of the case is avoided).
    • The second tripod should have the proud leg standing up (this allows the two sensor extension cords to be packed between the legs in that section)
